Dungeon layout: 
I know it's just the intro dungeon, but it's also going to be the only one in the demo. So to leave a good impression and to really build hype for the full game, we need to have a more complex, fully featured dungeon with less linear pathing, in itself being a larger overarching "puzzle" to figure out. As a Zelda dungeon connoisseur (lol) since the early 90s I could handle this part, while you focus on implementing individual puzzles.
Sub-points related to this:
We should have the multi-part gem-key door mechanic we previously discussed.
I'd LOVE to reuse the Junkrunner repair minigame in Spellcraft, it would be really cool to have all sorts of magical runestones, or ancient mechanical gears or pipes. One new feature I thought of was to have items that only fit on top of a previous item that the player has already put in place. Maybe items could be removable as well, for example say we had 3 different stones that need to be placed in a certain order according to hints, and they can be swapped around (we'll come up with something better, but just to illustrate).
I think the mechanic with removing water from vines to kill them or dry them out could also add some more depth to the dungeon.
We discussed wether bramble vines should hurt the player or not: I think we could have a purple "poison" vine that hurts you, while the green one is walkable/touchable.
It would be interesting if we could have some (possibly optional) element in the dungeon that relates to the outside, like maybe you need to talk to a townsperson to get their help for a certain thing, maybe you need an item that can only be found in the forest, and/or maybe there's something else that requires you to unlock a side entrance that leads to a part of the forest to do something there.
